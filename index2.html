ğŸ“œ Historical Significance

Lohagad, meaning "Iron Fort," has a rich history dating back to the Satavahana dynasty. It was captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1648 but had to be ceded to the Mughals under the Treaty of Purandar. The fort was recaptured in 1670 and remained under Maratha control until the British captured it in 1818.

1648: Captured by Shivaji Maharaj
1665: Ceded to Mughals (Treaty of Purandar)
1670: Recaptured by Marathas
1818: Captured by British East India Company
